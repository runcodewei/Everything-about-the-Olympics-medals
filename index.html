Everything about the Olympic medals

This visualisation provides some information on the Olympic medals in the recent Olympics: the 2020 Tokyo Summer Olympic Games and the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympic Games.

1. An overview of the medals distribution

Following two geographic maps display the total medals that each participating country earned. Hover over each country to see more details.

From the above, we can see that the national teams generally performed better in the Summer Games than the Winter Games, as there are more participating sports in the Summer Games than the Winter Games.

2. A close look at the top medal earning countries

Following are the top 10 countries that earned the most amount of medals in the Summer Games and the Winter Games respectively.

From the charts, we can see that the USA and China performed the best in the Summer Games, while Norway and Russia performed the best in the Winter Games.

3. Different aspects affecting the number of medals

Following two scatterplots outline some of the factors affecting the medal distribution.

The left graph shows that, in general, those countries with a relatively high amount of GDP per capita tend to perform better in the Olympics Games. However, there are two exceptions, both China and Russia have relatively small amount of GDP per capita, but performed quiet well.

The right graph demonstrates that there is a correlation between the performance in the Summer Games and the Winter Games, that is you can draw a diagonal line. Moreover, the national team tends to have similar rank for both games.

4. Different factors affecting the number of medals earned

Following two scatterplots outline some of the factors affecting the medal distribution.

Summer Games

From the charts, we can see that the USA is leading the Summer Games with the most amount of gold, silver, and bronze medals.

Winter Games

On the other side, the Winter Games is a little bit competitive. With Norway securing the most amount of gold medals, and Russia securing most amount of silver medals. Lastly, Russia and Canada both seucring the equal amount of bronze medals.

5. Conclusion

Overall, there are some differences between the Summer Games and the Winter Games. The Summer Games had more participating sports than the Winter Games, and it is easier to earn medals in the former than the latter. In addition, majority of the medal earning countries in the Winter Games were the ones with a good economy.
